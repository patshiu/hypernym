Hypernym’s mission: make the maintenance of interactive educational exhibits easier to sustain.

As exhibits in Math, Science, and even Art museums increasingly rely on digital and physical interactivity, planning for obsolescence is now more crucial than ever. The technologies used to build these exhibits, within only several years, may be discontinued or no longer supported.

Our goal is to identify sustainable exhibit design practices–techniques in fabrication, documentation, and preservation that consider the lifespans of of both hardware and software.

Our current research goal is to focus on creating tools for assessment.

Target Deliverables
  • Review of common points of failure in interactive exhibits
  • free-to-use exhibit usability audit sheet
  • free-to-use exhibit documentation checklist
  • free-to-use exhibit maintenance sustainability assessment

We are drawing upon our own professional experience in maintaining and designing interactive exhibits, and are seeking others in the field with whom we can conduct interviews.

If you would like to to chat with us or be notified when we publish our findings, do get in touch!

Jesse Harding

Jesse Harding is a fabricator, artist, designer, and educator with experience in traditional means of fabrication as well as the deployment of technology in interactive experiences. Jesse is currently a lecturer at Parsons School of Design in the Design + Technology program as well as a museum technician at The National Museum of Mathematics.

Pat Shiu

Pat Shiu is a design consultant with an expertise in user-centric design and the preservation of born-digital works. She is currently Associate Director of Design at Rhizome at the New Museum, where she leads design on Webrecorder, an open-source, free-to-use internet archiving tool. Outside of her work in the arts, Pat has led design teams in digital agencies and facilitated design workshops for clients in the financial, educational, and governmental sectors.
